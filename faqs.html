Frequently Asked Questions

Welcome to our Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ) page about the Balsam Fir Wreath industry in Maine! Maine’s iconic Balsam Fir trees are not only a hallmark of the state’s natural beauty, but they also play a central role in its economy, especially during the holiday season. For generations, Maine’s wreath makers have been crafting high-quality, fragrant Balsam Fir wreaths that have become a beloved tradition in homes across the country. Whether you're curious about the wreath-making process, the environmental benefits of Balsam Fir, or how Maine’s wreath industry supports local communities, this page will provide you with all the information you need.

1. Why are wreaths made in Maine so popular?
  • Aroma and Freshness: Balsam fir has a strong, fragrant aroma, which is why many people want Maine wreaths at Christmas time..
  • Strong Fir: Fir trees are plentiful in Maine and are considered some of the finest in the world. They grow better in the Maine climate than in other areas.
  • Sustainability: Maine fir growers make sure that when fir trees are used for Christmas wreath, new seedlings are planted to keep the resource plentiful.
2. When do wreath makers start working in Maine?
  • Harvesting: You can only harvest fir tips in the late fall (October-December), after needles are "set." Once the needles are "set", the balsam fir tree stops growing until later in the Spring. Balsam fir tips would lose their needles very quickly if the tips were harvested before this time. Local Mainers will tell 'ya' that you have two have at least three consecutive nights of below freezing temperatures before you can harvest fir tips.
  • Peak Season: Most wreath makers craft wreaths around the holiday months, particularly November and December, as they are used for Christmas decorations, but wreaths can be sold at other times for special occasions.
3. Where can I buy Maine wreaths?
  • Online: Many Maine wreath makers sell their wreaths directly in an online storefront.
  • Outdoor Markets: During the holiday season in Maine, many wreath crafters sell their product at holiday craft fairs. It would not be unusual to see roadside stands with wreath makers selling hand-made wreaths along with Christmas trees..
  • Local Supermarkets” Many of the local grocery stores (such as Hannaford and Shaw’s) sell Christmas wreaths along with other holiday greenery. You might also find Christmas wreaths in major stores such as Sam’s Club or Bj’s.
  • Specialty Stores: Local gift shops, home décor stores (such as Lowes or Home Depot), and garden centers (such as local nurseries) also sell Maine-made wreaths, especially during the holiday season.
4. How much will a Maine-made wreath cost me?
  • Wreath Prices: You might expect to pay from $20 to $50 for a 12-18-inch wreath. If you want something larger, you can expect to pay upwards of $100.
  • Decorated Wreaths: Wreaths with special decorations (e.g., ribbons, hand-crafted bows, snow-sprinkled pinecones, holiday berries) can cost anywhere from $40 to $100 or more, depending on the size and special design (crosses, candy-canes, or stars).
5. Are Maine wreaths okay for the environment?
  • Yes, most wreath makers in Maine use fir tips that come from trees that has been grown in Maine forests and decorate with biodegradable materials. Some wreaths are decorated with moss, pinecones, and fresh Maine berries. Pine or cedar tips are often added to the wreath to add texture and fragrance..
