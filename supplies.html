Wreath Making Supplies

Assembling the Supplies

To make a handcrafted Christmas wreath, you'll need the following supplies. Begin with a 12" crimped wire wreath ring, which serves as the base for your greenery. You'll also need fresh balsam fir branches (assembled into tip bundles), known for their soft, fragrant needles, to form the bulk of the wreath. Floral wire (typically 22-gauge) is necessary to secure the tip bundles to the ring, while wire cutters will help you trim both the wire and cut the branches into tip bundles. Protect your hands with garden gloves as balsam fir branches can be prickly. Finally, a wreath hanger or a decorative ribbton can be used to hang the finished wreath.

Wreath Making Supplies ×
Supply Description Image
Wreath Ring 12" crimped wire ring for 22" finished wreath -- other rings in 10" - 36" sizes can be used depending on the desired finished size.
Greenery The wreath is made with balsam fir branches, snipped to 4" to 6" tips and assembled into tip bundles. Cedar and/or pine branches can be intertwined to add texture and fragrance.
Wreath Wire Typically in a 22-gauge thickness. This wire is both strong enough to hold the branches securely in place and flexible enough to be easily manipulated and wrapped around the frame. The wire is usually green to blend in with the foliage.
Wire Cutters Wire cutters provide a clean cut for both the wire and any excess branches that need trimming.
Garden Gloves Garden gloves are an essential tool for crafting balsam fir wreaths, as they protect your hands from the sharp needles and rough branches of the fir.
